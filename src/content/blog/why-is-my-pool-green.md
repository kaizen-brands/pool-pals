A green pool doesn't happen overnight. It's usually the result of one (or more) of five things going wrong. Here's how to diagnose the problem — and what the fix looks like.

## 1. Insufficient chlorine

**The cause**: Chlorine kills algae. If your level drops below 1ppm, algae gets a foothold.

**The fix**: Shock the pool with 5–10x the normal chlorine dose. Run the filter 24/7 for 2–3 days. Test daily.

**Prevention**: Maintain 2–4ppm free chlorine at all times. In Queensland's UV, that means checking twice weekly in summer.

## 2. Poor filtration

**The cause**: Your filter is clogged, undersized, or not running long enough.

**The fix**: Backwash sand filters. Clean cartridge filters. Check your pump's flow rate — if it's dropped 20% from baseline, something's blocked.

**Prevention**: Run your pump 8–10 hours daily in summer. Backwash every 4–6 weeks.

## 3. Imbalanced pH

**The cause**: pH above 7.8 makes chlorine ineffective. pH below 7.2 corrodes equipment.

**The fix**: Test and adjust pH before adding any chemicals. Use soda ash to raise, muriatic acid to lower.

**Prevention**: Test weekly. Adjust promptly.

## 4. Phosphates

**The cause**: Phosphates are algae food. They enter via leaves, fertiliser runoff, and even some pool chemicals.

**The fix**: Use a phosphate remover. Then shock and filter intensively.

**Prevention**: Skim daily. Trim overhanging trees. Use a phosphate test monthly.

## 5. Cyanuric acid overload

**The cause**: Too much stabiliser (CYA) locks up your chlorine. It can't do its job.

**The fix**: There's no chemical remover. You must partially drain and refill.

**Prevention**: Keep CYA at 30–50ppm. Don't use stabilised chlorine tablets exclusively.
