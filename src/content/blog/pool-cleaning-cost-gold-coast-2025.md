If you're a Gold Coast pool owner, you've probably asked yourself: *how much should I actually be paying for pool cleaning?*

The short answer: most homeowners pay between **$45 and $75 per visit** for a standard weekly service. But there's more to it than a headline number.

## What affects pool cleaning cost?

### 1. Pool size
A small plunge pool (under 30,000L) takes 30–40 minutes to clean. A large resort-style pool (100,000L+) can take 90 minutes. Most services price by time, so bigger pools cost more.

### 2. Pool type
Salt water pools need less chemical balancing but more equipment attention. Mineral pools need specialty chemicals. Chlorine pools are the baseline.

### 3. Frequency
Weekly cleans are cheaper per visit than fortnightly because the pool doesn't get as dirty. One-off cleans (like after a storm) cost more because there's more work.

### 4. Extras
Heaters, water features, spa attachments — they all add time and complexity.

## Gold Coast price guide (2025)

| Service | Frequency | Typical Price |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| Basic clean | Weekly | $45–$55 |
| Full service (chemicals included) | Weekly | $55–$75 |
| Fortnightly clean | Fortnightly | $40–$50 |
| Green pool recovery | One-off | $129–$250 |
| Equipment repair | As needed | $80–$400 |

## Hidden costs to watch for

- **Chemicals**: Some services charge $15–$30 extra per visit for chemicals. Ask upfront.
- **Call-out fees**: Emergency visits can attract a $50–$80 surcharge.
- **Equipment markups**: Parts bought through your pool guy often have a 30–50% markup.
